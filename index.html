Mobile Legends

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang began as a mobile 5v5 MOBA built to deliver shorter, phonefriendly matches compared with PC MOBAs. It was released in 2016 under the Mobile Legends: 5v5 MOBA branding, then later reintroduced under the Bang Bang subtitle after early controversy about similarities to other MOBA elements. Over time it expanded its hero roster, modes, and regional servers, and it became one of the defining mobile esports titles in Southeast Asia.The game was created by Moonton, which started in Shanghai and first found success with Magic Rush: Heroes before moving into the MOBA space. MLs design and rollout benefited from Moontons focus on international markets, especially where mobilefirst gaming was already dominant. By 2017, it was seeing explosive popularity in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines, helping turn it into a mainstream everyday competitive game rather than just a niche MOBA.A major part of MLs early history is the legal and IP controversy around its resemblance to League of Legends. Riot Games filed a lawsuit in 2017, and while the U.S. case was dismissed on forum grounds, related actions continued elsewhere; Tencent Riots parent later won a separate case in Shanghai connected to Moontons CEO and a noncompete dispute, with damages awarded. These disputes pushed Moonton to adjust branding and sharpen MLBBs identity as its own product while it kept growing.

Heroes

In Mobile Legends, heroes are the playable characters you choose before each match, and each one is built around a specific kit usually a passive plus active skills/ultimate that defines how they fight, move, and contribute to team objectives. A big part of learning the game is understanding what your heros kit is meant to doburst damage, sustain in long fights, protect allies, control enemies, or scale into the late gamebecause that determines when you should fight, rotate, or focus on farming. The hero roster started small and has expanded continuously over the years as new heroes are added and older ones are adjusted for balance. For example, Liquipedia notes that as of Patch 2.1.40 there are 131 heroes, which shows how much the playable pool has grown and it keeps changing as patches arrive.Heroes are commonly grouped into core roles/classesTank, Fighter, Assassin, Mage, Marksman, and Supportbecause these classes describe the kind of job a hero usually does in a teamfight and across the match. If you want quick mental models, you can think of Franco as a Tank example, Balmond as a Fighter example, Hayabusa as an Assassin example, Nana as a Mage example, Layla as a Marksman example, and Rafaela as a Support exampleand there are also hybrid heroes like Kimmy that blend roles.

Actual matches, hero choice is also tied to lane/job assignments like EXP Lane, Mid Lane, Roam, Jungle, and Gold Lane, because each position has different priorities wave clear and rotations, protecting the carry, securing objectives, or accelerating farm. The same hero class doesnt always mean the same position every gamesome Tanks roam, some Fighters jungle, some Mages mid, some Marksmen gold, and some Supports can flexso it helps to learn both what my hero does and where my hero is supposed to be most of the time. ML heroes also have a strong identity/lore angle, and Moonton Moonton has included characters inspired by Southeast Asian history and folklore to resonate with regional audiencesexamples often mentioned include LapuLapu Philippines, Kadita Indonesia folklore inspiration, and Gatotkaca drawn from myth/legend. This is why a lot of players connect not just to the gameplay kit, but also to a heros theme, voice lines, and skins.

Skills

In Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, a skill usually means the unique abilities built into each heros kit. Most heroes have an alwayson passive innate ability, two regular skills, and one ultimate, although a few heroes have special exceptions like extra normal skills or unusual upgrade rules. Your heros skills are what define your role in fightswhether youre controlling enemies, protecting allies, bursting targets, or sustaining through damage.During a match, you level up by gaining EXP, and that lets you unlock and upgrade your skills. In general, your ultimate becomes available at Level 4 and can be upgraded multiple times as you level with a few hero exceptions that learn their ultimate differently. This hit Level 4 power spike is why players often prioritize fast early farming/rotationsbecause many heroes become dramatically more dangerous the moment their ultimate is online. Skills are also balanced around cooldowns and resource costs. Many heroes spend mana, while some use energy a yellow bar instead; energy typically has a smaller max amount but regenerates faster, and according to the community wiki it generally isnt increased by items/emblems the way other stats can be. Understanding your resource type matters a lotmana heroes often need timing and conservation early, while energy heroes can sometimes trade more aggressively but must manage bursts and regeneration windows.How you aim skills is just as important as what the skill does. ML has settings like Smart Targeting and Hero Lock that can help you cast more accurately in teamfights; with Smart Targeting, you can presshold a skill and drag the targeting dot toward the enemy you want to hit, instead of relying purely on autoaim. Players often tweak these control options so their stuns, hooks, and longrange shots dont accidentally go to the wrong target. Finally, many players also call Battle Spells a skill because its an extra active ability on top of your hero kit. You pick one Battle Spell before the match starts, and you cant change it midgame, so choosing the right spell Flicker, Retribution, Purify, etc. is part of your overall skill planusually to cover your heros weakness or amplify their strength.
